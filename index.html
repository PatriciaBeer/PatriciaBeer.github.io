About me

Hi, I am a docotral student in Computational Linguistics at the University of Tübingen. With a background in language studies, I enjoy using computational analysis tools to learn more about language and extract information about language characteristics from large corpora.

In the first phase of my thesis project, I combined parsing and selectional preferences to improve parser performance on ambiguity solving tasks such as PP attachment and subject-object resolution for German and Dutch. The current second half of my thesis is devoted to neural network model introspection across different model architectures. I created large task-specific test suites (SORTS for subject-object resolution and PPATS for PP attachment) with detailed linguistic annotations to test the capabilities of parser architectures ranging from traditional Chen-and-Manning-style parsers to BiLSTMs and language models such as BERT or XLM-RoBERTa.
